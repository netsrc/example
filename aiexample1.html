Following in the footsteps of our founders and their deep commitment to Catholic faith and American values, our principles are guided by a fundamental belief in traditional Christian teaching, individual dignity, equality of opportunity, self-sufficiency, hard work, and faithful formation.

Based on this vision, the Strake Foundation pursues a mission to support conservative Catholic causes, preserve the heritage of George W. Strake and his family, educate individuals and communities in truth, and care for those who are ailing — honoring a legacy of integrity and generous stewardship that spans more than seventy years.

Grant Applications

Before You Apply

The Strake Foundation distributes approximately $3 million annually across roughly 400 requests. Only organizations whose mission aligns with the Foundation's traditional Catholic values and donor intent will be considered.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Must be a 501(c)(3) tax-exempt organization
  • Must be aligned with traditional Catholic or conservative Christian values
  • Must not support woke, DEI, or ideologically contrary initiatives
  • Must represent causes George W. Strake Sr. or Jr. would have directly endorsed
  • Priority given to Catholic education, healthcare, and religious formation
  • Care for ailing and dying members of the faithful community is a priority

How to Apply

  • Step 1: Download and complete the PDF application form below
  • Step 2: Gather the 5 required supporting documents
  • Step 3: Submit completed application + all documents via email to the Foundation
  • Step 4: Await review by the Foundation Board
⬇ Download Application (PDF) Email Submission Instructions

Required Supporting Documents

  • IRS determination letter confirming 501(c)(3) status
  • Most recent audited financial statements
  • Current fiscal year budget
  • Project or program narrative
  • Letter of endorsement from a Catholic pastor or bishop (if applicable)

What We Do Not Fund

  • Woke or DEI initiatives of any kind
  • Organizations contrary to traditional Catholic or Christian teaching
  • Causes George Sr. or George Jr. Strake would not have endorsed
  • Political campaigns or lobbying
  • Individuals (grants are made to organizations only)

Frequently Asked Questions

When does the Foundation accept applications?
Applications are accepted on a rolling basis throughout the year. The Foundation board reviews requests and makes grant decisions quarterly.
How large are typical grants?
Grant sizes vary widely depending on the project and organizational need. The Foundation distributes approximately $3 million per year across roughly 400 requests.
Do you fund individuals?
No. Grants are made only to qualified 501(c)(3) tax-exempt organizations. Individual requests are not considered.
Can non-Catholic organizations apply?
Organizations grounded in traditional Bible-based Christianity may apply, but priority is given to conservative Catholic organizations whose mission aligns with the Strake family's donor intent.
How are decisions made?
All applications are reviewed by the Foundation Board, which evaluates each request against the Strake family's donor intent, values, and mission priorities.

Grant Reports

Grantee organizations are required to submit a brief grant report upon completion of funded projects. Report forms are provided to grantees at the time of the award. Please contact the Foundation for more information.

Mission

Building on the Strake family's belief in the transformative power of faith and faithful formation, the Foundation strengthens communities, preserves the heritage of Texas and the Catholic Church, and honors every person's God-given dignity.

The George W. Strake Foundation advances the vision of its founders through dedication to traditional Catholic beliefs and conservative American values, specifically:

Traditional Catholic Faith • Conservative Stewardship
Individual Dignity • Faithful Formation
Self-Sufficiency • Hard Work • Resourcefulness

What the Foundation Funds

  • Traditional Catholic and conservative Christian organizations
  • Catholic education, schools, and universities
  • Faithful formation programs and human development
  • Care for ailing and dying members of the faithful community
  • Preservation of the Strake family legacy and Texas heritage
  • Programs honoring the memory of George Sr., George Jr., and Susan Keough Strake

What the Foundation Does Not Fund

  • Woke or DEI initiatives of any kind
  • Causes contrary to traditional Catholic or Christian teaching
  • Organizations misrepresenting the values of the founding Strake family

In Memoriam

The Honorable George William Strake, Jr.
June 10, 1935 – 2024

A devoted husband, father, businessman, and philanthropist whose Catholic faith shaped every aspect of his life. George Jr. served as President of the Strake Foundation and as Texas Secretary of State, carrying his family's legacy of service, faith, and integrity into the twenty-first century.

George W. Strake, Sr.
1894 – 1969

Oilman, Catholic philanthropist, and the founder of the Strake Foundation. George Sr.'s discovery of the Conroe Oil Field and his deep devotion to the Church defined a legacy of faith and generous stewardship that continues to guide the Foundation today.

Our Legacy

George W. Strake was an oilman and philanthropist who left the majority of his estate to the Foundation when he died in 1969. The Foundation is supported by Mr. Strake's endowment as well as by funds from the estate of Mrs. Susan K. Strake, who died in 1975, and gifts from George W. Strake, Jr., who died in 2024.

Together, their lives reflect a legacy of faith and generous stewardship that continues to guide the mission of the Strake Foundation today.

George W. Strake, Sr. (1894–1969)

Born in 1894, George W. Strake rose from modest beginnings to become a significant figure in the Texas oil industry. After serving in World War I, he entered the petroleum business at a time when wildcatters and independent producers were shaping the economic future of Texas.

Beyond his success in oil, George W. Strake was deeply guided by his Catholic faith. His commitment to the Church and to charitable causes shaped both his personal life and his philanthropic vision.

Susan Keough Strake (d. 1975)

Mrs. Susan K. Strake shared her husband's devotion to faith and service. After Mr. Strake's death, she continued their charitable efforts and ensured that the family's philanthropic commitments would endure. Her estate further strengthened the Foundation's resources.

George W. Strake, Jr. (1935–2024)

George W. Strake, Jr. carried forward this legacy into the twenty-first century. Through his own generosity and leadership, he reinforced the family's tradition of stewardship and thoughtful giving. He served as Texas Secretary of State and was a graduate of the University of Notre Dame and Harvard Business School.

Strake became closely associated with the development of the Conroe Oil Field, which would become one of the most important oil discoveries in the state's history.

Early in his career, Strake's ambitions took him to Cuba, where he leased land for drilling. The venture went bankrupt — a defining setback that sharpened his geological instincts. Returning to the United States, he applied his hard-won expertise in topography and geology to the fields of Texas.

The discovery and expansion of the Conroe field in the 1930s shaped the regional economy and established Conroe as a vital energy hub. The Great Conroe Oil Field documents this chapter of Texas history — a story of resourcefulness, risk, and reward that mirrors the values of the Strake Foundation itself.

Learn More About the Conroe Field

The Fisherman's Tomb recounts the archaeological discovery of what is believed to be the burial site of Saint Peter beneath St. Peter's Basilica in Rome. The book highlights themes of enduring faith, perseverance, and the historical foundations of Christian belief.

These values resonated strongly with the Strake family and influenced their commitment to supporting religious, educational, and community initiatives. The depth of George W. Strake Sr.'s Catholic faith is reflected in this work — a faith that built the Foundation and continues to define its mission.

Today, the Strake Foundation honors this enduring faith, grounded in a history of integrity, hard work, and devotion to the Church.
