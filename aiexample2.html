About the Foundation

Our Mission & Values

The Strake Foundation is a private charitable foundation established in 1952 and operating since 1956, grounded in the Catholic faith and conservative values of its founders.

George W. Strake was an oilman and philanthropist who left the majority of his estate to the Foundation when he died in 1969. The Foundation is supported by Mr. Strake's endowment, funds from the estate of Mrs. Susan K. Strake (d. 1975), and gifts from George W. Strake, Jr. (d. 2024).

The Foundation handles approximately 400 requests per year and distributes roughly $3 million annually, funded primarily by oil production income of approximately 10,000 barrels per day.

The Strake Family

Three Generations of Faithful Stewardship

George W. Strake, Sr.
1894 – 1969 · Founder

Born in 1894, George Strake rose from modest beginnings to become a defining figure in the Texas oil industry. After serving in World War I, he entered the petroleum business, applying hard-won geological expertise to discover the Great Conroe Oil Field — one of the most significant oil discoveries in Texas history. His Catholic faith shaped every dimension of his life and his philanthropic vision.

Susan Keough Strake
d. 1975 · Patron

Mrs. Susan K. Strake shared her husband's devotion to faith and service. After Mr. Strake's death in 1969, she continued their charitable efforts and ensured the family's philanthropic commitments would endure. Her estate further strengthened the Foundation's resources, allowing it to expand its charitable reach.

George W. Strake, Jr.
June 10, 1935 – 2024 · Legacy Guardian

A graduate of Notre Dame and Harvard Business School, George Jr. served as Texas Secretary of State (1979) and Chairman of the Republican Party of Texas (1983–1988). He carried his family's legacy of Catholic faith, service, and conservative stewardship into the twenty-first century, supporting and guiding the Foundation until his death in 2024.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Must be a qualified non-profit organization tax exempt under Section 501(c)(3) of the IRS Code
  • No grants are made to individuals
  • Organization's mission must align with the Foundation's Donor Intent Guidelines
  • No support for woke or DEI initiatives contrary to the Foundation's values
